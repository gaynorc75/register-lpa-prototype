After the lasting power of attorney is registered

When the LPA is registered, the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG) will notify:

  • the donors
  • the attorneys
  • anyone the donor has chosen to receive updates on their behalf, known as a ‘correspondent’
  • anyone the donor has asked to be told about their LPA, known as ‘people to notify’

When you can use the LPA

You can only use the LPA when it’s been registered.

Health and welfare LPAs can only be used when the donor no longer has the mental capacity to make their own decisions.

Property and affairs LPAs can be used as soon as they are registered while the donor has mental capacity and both:

  • the LPA says they can
  • the donor gives permission

This could be on a short-term basis, for example when the donor’s on holiday, or to provide long-term help.

When making decisions on behalf of the donor, the attorney needs to prove they’re named on the LPA.

Changes you need to report

You need to report changes to OPG LINK HERE such as if the donor changes their contact details or an attorney dies.

As long as the donor still has mental capacity, they can remove certain attorneys.

When the LPA ends

There are several reasons an LPA will end LINK HERE including if:

  • the donor dies
  • an attorney cannot act and there's no replacement attorney

The donor can also cancel the whole LPA, whether it’s been registered or not, as long as they have mental capacity to do so.
